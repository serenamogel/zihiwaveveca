100 years old American Essayist Is Married Third Times. Know About His Marital Life

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Friday, July 19, 2024

Roger Angell is an American essayist who specializes in sports writing, particularly baseball. He has been a regular contributor to The New Yorker for many years and served as its top fiction editor. 

During his active career, he has published various pieces of fiction, nonfiction, and criticism, as well as an annual Christmas poem for The New Yorker for many years. He has been awarded several awards, including George Polk Award, J.G. Taylor Spink Award, and many more from his tremendous contribution. 

Fortunate Marital Life with Wife, Margaret Moorman

One hundred years old, Roger has been married three times in his entire life. He has exchanged his wedding vow with his wife, Margaret Moorman. Margaret is an American writer and teacher, according to the Ellsworth American Newspaper. She is the third wife of the legendary writer.

In 2014, the old duo accepted one another as husband and wife. The same year he released his essay under the name This Old Man which addresses ageing, sex, and death. As of now, Mr. and Mrs. Angell are enjoying a blissful marital life together for seven years. 

Another sports writer, Bob Ryan is also a happily married man.

Previously Married to Ex-Spouse, Carol Rogge Angell

Prior to accepting Maragaret as the love of his life, Roger was married to Carol Rogge Angell. They got married back in 1964 in an intimate ceremony. However, their relationship ends on 10th April 2012 due to the death of Carol. 

They remained married for forty-eight years until Rogge took her last breath at the age of seventy-three. The reason for her death was metastatic breast cancer. They have one son, John Henry Angell, who is fully grown up and is living in Portland, Oregon.

Roger Angell's Bio/Wiki

Roger Angell was born on 19th September 1920 in New York, United States. His parents are Katharine Sergeant Angell White, the legendary fiction editor, a renowned essayist and Ernest Angell, an attorney and headed American Civil Liberties Union. 

Moreover, he is the stepson of the writer, E.B. White. Furthermore, he graduated from Pomfret School and later attended Harvard University. During World War II, he even served for United States Army Air Forces.
